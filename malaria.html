Understanding Malaria

What is Malaria?

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites that are transmitted to people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Common Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Shaking Chills
  • Headache
  • Muscle Aches
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea and Vomiting

Prevention

  • Use insecticide-treated bed nets (ITNs).
  • Apply insect repellent to skin and clothing.
  • Take antimalarial drugs as prescribed by a healthcare professional.
  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and trousers, especially during dusk and dawn when mosquitoes are most active.
  • Spray insecticide in your home.

Basic First Aid/What to Do

If you suspect you have malaria, seek medical attention immediately. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial. Avoid self-medication.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth:

Only children get malaria.

Fact:

Malaria can affect people of all ages.

Myth:

Malaria is contagious like a cold.

Fact:

Malaria is transmitted through mosquito bites, not through direct person-to-person contact.
