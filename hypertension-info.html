Understanding Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

What is Hypertension?

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high. Usually, hypertension is defined as blood pressure above 140/90, and is considered severe if the pressure is above 180/120.

Common Symptoms

  • Severe Headaches
  • Nosebleeds
  • Fatigue
  • Confusion
  • Vision Problems
  • Chest Pain
  • Difficulty Breathing

Prevention

  • Eat a healthy diet low in salt.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Limit alcohol consumption.
  • Do not smoke.
  • Manage stress effectively.

Basic Management/What to Do

Managing hypertension often involves regular monitoring of blood pressure, adopting a healthy lifestyle, and taking prescribed medications as directed by a healthcare professional. It's crucial to adhere to the treatment plan to prevent serious complications.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth:

You can always feel if your blood pressure is high.

Fact:

Many people have high blood pressure without experiencing any symptoms, which is why it's often called the "silent killer."

Myth:

Once you start taking blood pressure medication, you can stop if your pressure goes down.

Fact:

Hypertension often requires lifelong management, and stopping medication without a doctor's advice can be dangerous.
