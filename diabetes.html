Understanding Diabetes

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (blood sugar), which leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys, and nerves. Several mechanisms contribute to hyperglycemia, but most common is impaired insulin production, ineffective action of insulin, or both.

Common Symptoms

  • Frequent Urination (Polyuria)
  • Excessive Thirst (Polydipsia)
  • Unexplained Weight Loss
  • Increased Hunger (Polyphagia)
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred Vision
  • Slow-Healing Sores

Prevention

  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Eat a healthy diet.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Avoid smoking.
  • Manage stress.

Basic Management/What to Do

If you are diagnosed with diabetes, it's crucial to follow your healthcare provider's recommendations. This may include adhering to a meal plan, monitoring blood sugar levels regularly, taking prescribed medications (oral medications or insulin), and engaging in regular exercise.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth:

Diabetes is caused by eating too much sugar.

Fact:

While diet plays a role, type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease, and type 2 diabetes is influenced by genetics, lifestyle, and other factors.

Myth:

People with diabetes can't eat any sweets.

Fact:

Sweets can be part of a balanced diet in moderation, especially when planned with a healthcare professional.
